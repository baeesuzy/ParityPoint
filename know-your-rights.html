Workplace Safety

Every employee has the right to a safe and healthy workplace. Employers must follow safety laws and provide training, equipment, and a hazard-free environment.

  • Safe working conditions and proper equipment
  • Training on workplace hazards
  • Right to report unsafe conditions
  • Protection from retaliation for reporting

Harassment

Harassment in the workplace is illegal and can take many forms, including verbal, physical, or visual conduct that creates a hostile environment. You have the right to work free from harassment.

  • Unwelcome comments or jokes
  • Physical intimidation or threats
  • Offensive images or emails
  • Sexual advances or requests

Anti-Discrimination Laws

Anti-discrimination laws protect you from unfair treatment based on race, gender, age, disability, and more. Learn your rights and how to take action if you experience discrimination at work.

  • Report discrimination to HR or a supervisor.
  • Keep records of incidents and communications.
  • Seek support from advocacy groups or legal counsel.
  • Learn more about anti-discrimination laws

Equal Pay Rights

Equal pay laws ensure that employees performing substantially similar work receive equal compensation, regardless of gender or other protected characteristics.

  • You have the right to discuss wages with coworkers.
  • Request pay transparency and fair pay audits.
  • Report pay disparities to HR or relevant authorities.
  • Learn more about Equal Pay Rights laws

Workplace Safety

You have the right to a safe and healthy workplace. Employers must provide proper training, equipment, and address hazards promptly.

  • Report unsafe conditions to your employer or OSHA.
  • Participate in safety training and use provided equipment.
  • You may refuse dangerous work if it violates safety laws.
  • Learn more about Workplace Safety

Harassment

Harassment includes unwelcome comments, conduct, or advances that create a hostile work environment. You have the right to work free from harassment.

  • Report harassment to HR or management immediately.
  • Document all incidents and communications.
  • Seek support from trusted colleagues or legal resources.
  • Learn more about Harassment laws
